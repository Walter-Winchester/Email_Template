The legendary investor John Templeton once said: “Bull markets are born on pessimism, grown on scepticism, mature on optimism, and die on euphoria”. With the benefit of hindsight, pessimism and scepticism featured big for much of 2020 in the aftermath of the Covid-led market crisis. Yet the market recovery from the depths of the crisis last March has been far swifter than anyone expected — in sharp contrast to the post-GFC recovery. This leaves most wondering whether we have swiftly moved past the maturing stage of the bull market and are dancing dangerously on the edge of euphoria.
